# Week 2 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, there is **no single right answer** — the point is to *decide* and *justify*, not to follow steps. Budget ~2.5 hours total.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Untangle the branches](challenge-01-untangle-branches.md)** — you inherit a repo with a knot of half-merged, diverged branches. Turn it into a clean, readable history without losing work.
2. **[Challenge 2 — Choose a merge strategy](challenge-02-choose-a-merge-strategy.md)** — given three realistic scenarios, pick merge vs. rebase vs. fast-forward-only and defend each choice in writing.

## How these are judged

Exercises check that you *can operate* Git. Challenges check that you can *reason* with it. For each challenge you'll produce a short write-up (`SOLUTION.md`) explaining not just what you did but **why** — the trade-offs you weighed and what you deliberately chose *not* to do.

A good submission:

- Reaches a clean end state (or a clearly justified one).
- Explains the reasoning, not just the commands.
- Names at least one alternative you rejected and why.
- Doesn't lose any committed work (check with `git reflog` if unsure — nothing should truly vanish).

Do Challenge 1 first — it gives you the mess that Challenge 2 asks you to reason about in the abstract.
