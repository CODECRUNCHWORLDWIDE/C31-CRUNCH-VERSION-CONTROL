# Week 5 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. There's no single right answer — you're being judged on **judgment**, not on hitting an exact command. Both are things you'll do for real on any team.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Split a giant PR into a reviewable stack](challenge-01-split-a-giant-pr.md)** — take one intimidating 900-line diff and turn it into a stack of small PRs nobody's afraid to review.
2. **[Challenge 2 — Navigate a contentious review to a good outcome](challenge-02-navigate-a-contentious-review.md)** — a review has gone sideways. Steer it back to a merged, better change without it turning personal.

Optional but strongly recommended. The gap between "can open a PR" and "can be trusted with a team's codebase" lives in exactly these two skills: making work reviewable, and handling disagreement well.

Do them after the three exercises. Each expects you're comfortable with the full open → review → merge loop.
