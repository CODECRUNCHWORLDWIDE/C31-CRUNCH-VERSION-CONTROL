# Week 8 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — you're judged on the quality of your reasoning, the completeness of your policy, and whether someone else could actually follow what you produced.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Migrate to trunk](challenge-01-migrate-to-trunk.md)** — take a chaotic repo drowning in long-lived branches and move it to trunk-based development without losing work or stopping the team.
2. **[Challenge 2 — Monorepo contribution policy](challenge-02-monorepo-policy.md)** — design the contribution rules for a multi-team monorepo so it scales instead of seizing up.

## How these are graded

Each challenge lists an explicit **"How success is judged"** section. In general: a strong submission states its assumptions, makes a defensible decision, names the trade-offs it accepted, and produces an artifact (a migration plan, a policy doc) concrete enough to hand to a real team. A weak submission is vague, ignores the messy constraints, or produces something no one could execute.

Do at least one; do both if you want the capstone to feel easy. They feed directly into the mini-project runbook.
